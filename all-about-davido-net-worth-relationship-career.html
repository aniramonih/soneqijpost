All About Davido | Net Worth, Relationship, Career

Davido is a Nigerian singer, songwriter, and record producer. He released an EP called “Son of Mercy,” as well as studio albums called “Omo Baba Olowo” (2012), “A Good Time” (2019), and “A Better Time” (2020). Davido is also well-known for mixing traditional African musical elements with worldwide modern pop, and he has produced several popular singles.

He established the music label Davido Music Worldwide (DMW). The English-language monthly news magazine New African, located in London, named him one of the Top 100 Most Influential Africans in 2019.

Where was Davido born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Davido was born on November 21, 1992, in Atlanta, Georgia, USA. The man’s full name is David Adedeji Adeleke. He is of American and Nigerian origin and follows the Christian faith. His zodiac sign is Scorpio.

His ancestors are also Black and Nigerian. Davido’s mother, Vero Adeleke, was a university professor, and his father, Adedeji Adeleke, is a business magnate. He is his father’s second son and the youngest of five children. In 2021, he will be 29 years old.

Davido attended the British International School in Lagos, Nigeria, before returning to the United States at the age of 16 to pursue business management at Oakwood University in Alabama.

He began his first music career after purchasing musical equipment and developing beats while attending Oakwood. He dropped out of Oakwood University and relocated to London to work on his voice to pursue music full-time.

In 2011, he returned to Nigeria. He also decided to honor his father by taking a break from music and enrolling at BDavidoock University. In addition, in July 2015, he obtained a music degree from BDavidoock after his father supported the school to create a music department for a single-student inaugural class.

How did Davido start his Professional Career?

Davido began working on his debut studio album, “Omo Baba Olowo,” in 2011. Davido’s production of the album’s debut single, “Back When,” featuring Naeto C, was made available on May 7, 2011. Shizzi and Davido cooperated on “Dami Duro,” the album’s second single, which was released on October 30, 2011.

The third single from the album, “Ekuro,” was released on January 25, 2012. The fourth single from the album, “Overseas,” was released on May 6, 2012. “All of You,” the album’s sixth single, was released on September 28, 2012. On February 25, 2013, he released the Shizzi-produced single “Gobe.” His second single, “Skelewu,” was released on August 13, 2013.

On February 1st, 2014, he released “Aye,” which T-Spiz produced. In June 2014, he collaborated on “Africa Rising” with Mi Casa, Lola Rae, Sarkodie, Diamond Platnumz, and Tiwa Savage. He later collaborated with Meek Mill on the trap track “Fans Mi,” which was released in 2015. His 20-track album, The Baddest, was set to be published in June 2015.

A few months after signing with Sony, he founded the record label Davido Music Worldwide (DMW). In July 2016, Davido and RCA Records agreed to a record contract. He released the 5-song EP “Son of Mercy” in October 2016. Tekno, who created “If,” released it to the public on February 17, 2017.
