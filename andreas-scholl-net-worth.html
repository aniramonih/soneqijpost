Andreas Scholl Overview and Wiki

Embarking on a Musical Journey: Early Performances

The journey of Andreas Scholl, a name synonymous with excellence in Baroque music, began in the quaint town of Rüdesheim where, in 1988, he first performed Bach’s Christmas Oratorio. This marked the beginning of a series of performances that would not only define his career but also reshape the landscape of classical music.

By 1991, Scholl was captivating audiences with his renditions of Bach’s St John Passion, conducted by none other than Philippe Herreweghe in Antwerp—a collaboration that signaled his rising prominence in the classical music world.

January 1993 saw Scholl stepping in for René Jacobs at the Théâtre Grévin in Paris, an event that proved pivotal, establishing him as a sensation in the music scene. The performance, where he was accompanied by harpsichordist Markus Märkl, set the stage for a lasting musical partnership that would endure for years.

A Flourishing Opera Career

Scholl’s opera career is particularly notable for his performances in roles originally written for the 18th-century alto castrato Senesino. His operatic breakthrough came in 1998 at Glyndebourne, where he performed as Bertarido in Handel’s Rodelinda.

This role, which he reprised in subsequent years, earned him critical acclaim, with reviews in the Sunday Times highlighting his ability to stop the show and create a trance-like experience for the audience with his rendition of Dove Sei.

Concert Tours and Notable Performances

Throughout his career, Scholl has not only excelled in opera but also made significant contributions to concert music. His performances with the Netherlands Bach Society and at prestigious venues like Avery Fisher Hall, where he performed Bach’s Christmas Oratorio, have been pivotal.

In 1999, Scholl embarked on a tour that included performances of Bach’s Mass in B minor across Japan, showcasing his deep connection with Bach’s music. He continued to explore this repertoire with various ensembles, bringing Baroque music to audiences worldwide.
