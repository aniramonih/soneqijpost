    Ben Stiller The Fact

  • He once had the opportunity to work on the popular sketch comedy show Saturday Night Live, based on The Money Hustler, his short parody of the film, The Color of Money.
  • In 1989, he began working as a performer and writer with Saturday Night Live . However, since the show was not interested in his short films, he chose to leave after working for 4 episodes.
  • In 2002, Ben Stiller was ranked 68th in Premiere’s Power 100 list .
  • He owns a production company called Red Hour Films whose name is inspired by an alien insurgency in The Return of the Archons episode of Star Trek .
  • Before he started acting, one of his first jobs in show business was as an intern on the Alan Thicke Dick of the Night show .
  • Stiller was primarily considered for the role of Willy Wonka in Charlie and the Chocolate Factory . However, Johnny Depp eventually managed to bag the role.
  • In 2005, he revealed that he suffered from a bipolar disorder that ran in his family.
  • He was diagnosed with prostate cancer in 2014, but his diagnosis wasn’t made public until 2016. After treatment, however, he was declared cancer-free.
  • In the late ’70s, Stiller performed regularly with NYC First All Children’s Theater , a New York City troupe. This was his first big discovery as an actor.
  • During his school days, he served as the drummer for the punk band called Death Penalty , which released his studio album Roadkill in 1982.
  • In 1986, he rose to prominence with his performance in the Broadway revival of John Guare’s The House of Blue Leaves. The production won 4 Tony Awards .
  • His first major commercial theatrical success was the 1994 romantic comedy Reality Bites. Besides one of the main roles in the film, he also served as the film’s director. He had also worked with screenwriter Helen Childress for about a year and a half to determine the story and setting for the project.
  • His famous character, Derek Zoolander, first appeared in a 1996 short film he made for the VH1 Fashion Awards . Zoolander was introduced in the film as a male model.
  • In 2001, he created a comedy film about his popular character and titled it: Zoolander. In addition to his main role, he was the producer, director and screenwriter of the project.
  • Ben has done extensive activism work for charities such as the Elizabeth Glaser Pediatric AIDS Foundation , Explain Yourself , and the Starlight Starbright Children’s Foundation .
  • In July 2018 he became Appointed goodwill ambassadors for UNHCR , the UN’s refugee arm.
