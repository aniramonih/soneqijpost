Breece Hall Parents- Father Raquel Guerra &amp; Mother Larhonda McDaniel

In 2021, Iowa State Cyclones star Breece Hall concluded the regular season in extraordinary fashion.

Hall became the first player since 1996 to accumulate over 250 scrimmage yards and score four touchdowns in a single game.

Moreover, he etched his name in the NCAA record books by scoring a rushing touchdown in 24 consecutive games, surpassing the previous record held by former standout Bill Burnett.

Hall’s exceptional performance propelled Iowa State to a resounding 48-14 victory over TCU, solidifying his status as a key figure in the team’s success.

However, it was a heartwarming moment shared with his family that captured the attention of media outlets and fans alike, overshadowing even his impressive on-field achievements.

Emotions ran high as Hall made his way toward his family at the Jack Trice Stadium.

Overwhelmed with a mixture of joy, pride, and gratitude, he embraced his mother, Larhonda. The significance of the moment was not lost on Larhonda, who herself was moved to tears by her son’s heartfelt gesture.

In the midst of the celebratory atmosphere, Larhonda shared her thoughts with Des Moines Register columnist Randy Peterson, expressing her overwhelming pride and admiration for Breece.

She described the moment as an overwhelming culmination of his hard work and dedication, not only for himself but also for his team and the loyal fans who had supported him throughout his collegiate career.

Sister Naria Hall

Naria Hall, the elder sister of Breece Hall, attended Wichita South High School.

After completing her high school education, she went on to pursue her college education at Friends University.

During her time at Friends University, Naria was an active member of the Women’s basketball team.

Naria had the opportunity to play for the Friends University Women’s basketball team for two seasons. She played during the 2018-2019 and 2019-2020 seasons.

As a dedicated player, Naria contributed her skills and talents to the team as a forward-position basketball player.

Standing at a height of 5’8″ tall, she brought her athleticism and versatility to the court, making valuable contributions to her team’s performance.
