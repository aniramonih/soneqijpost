Chaka Khan’s Career

American singer Chaka Khan began her career with Rufus in 1970; she was the band’s lead singer.

The Rufus band were famous all over the world for their prominent R&B and Funk music.

Further, Chaka and the Rufus dominated the 70s R&B with their several hit albums, including Rufusized, Rags to Rufus, Ask Rufus, Masterjam.

Although Chaka was dominating the music industry with Rufus, she decided to leave the band in 1978 in order to pursue a solo career.

In the same year, she released her debut solo album entitled I’m Every Woman.

Furthermore, her debut album sold over a million copies and was indeed certified platinum by RIAA.

Subsequently, she was featured in Stuff Like That song by Quincy Jones in 1978.

After a year gap, she rejoined Rufus to release a hit song entitled Do You Love What You Feel.

Her sixth studio album entitled “I Feel for You” was released in 1984.

Moreover, the title track became her signature song and rose her to international fame.

Similarly, “I Feel for You” topped the U.S. and U.K. R&B charts for the 26 weeks in a row.

Besides, she was named the best female R&B Vocal Performance for the song “I Feel For You.”

However, the 1990s was the decline decade for her as she managed to release one album called “Come 2 My House” with NPG Records.

In 2007, the R&B star Chaka made a stunning comeback with the album “Funk This,” a Grammy-winning album.

In November 2019, Chaka Khan collaborated with the famous modern-day pop star Ariana Grande for the song “Nobody” for Charlie’s Angels’ soundtrack.
