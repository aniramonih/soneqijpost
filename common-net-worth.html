Common Net Worth: Bio, Music, Poetry, Activism and Life

Common, an American rapper, musician, actor, and entrepreneur, has collaborated with some of the music industry’s biggest stars.

Apart from his music career, he’s acted in numerous blockbuster films alongside top Hollywood actors in various genres.

As of October 2023, Common’s estimated net worth stands at $45 million.

Biography

Common, whose birth name is Lonnie Rashid Lynn Jr., was born on March 13, 1972, making him 51 years old. He entered the world in Chicago, Illinois, at the Chicago Osteopathic Hospital.

Growing up, his family consisted of his father, Mahalia Ann Hines, who served as an educator and former principal at John Hope College Preparatory High School.

His father was also an ABA basketball player. When Common was six years old, his parents separated, and he was primarily raised by his mother.

However, his father remained a part of his life and even helped him secure employment in the early stages of his career.

Common began his journey in rap within his local community, and he eventually transitioned to larger projects.

In addition to his music career, he has gained recognition as a talented actor, delivering outstanding performances in the entertainment industry.

What is Common Net Worth?

Common, the American musician and actor, boasts a net worth of $45 million.

His major-label studio album, “Like Water for Chocolate,” marked a significant financial success, catapulting him to international acclaim and fame.

Beyond his music, Common has forged a prosperous career encompassing acting, activism, and endorsements.

Career

In the early 1990s, Common embarked on his rap career, releasing his debut album “Can I Borrow a Dollar?” in 1992.

His breakthrough came with his second album, “Resurrection,” in 1994. Since then, he has launched more than a dozen albums and engaged in numerous collaborations with fellow musicians.

In addition to his music career, Common has ventured into acting, appearing in various films and television shows such as “Smokin’ Aces,” “American Gangster,” “Selma,” and “The Chi.”

Common Net Worth: Other Ventures and Activism

In 2006, he took on a role as a Gap model and went on to endorse products for Microsoft, Diesel, BlackBerry, and NBA 2K8.

His first memoir, titled “One Day It’ll All Make Sense,” hit bookshelves in 2011. Subsequently, he published his second memoir, “Let Love Have the Last Word,” in May 2019.

Activism

Common actively supports animal rights and is a supporter of PETA. He participated in a PETA print ad campaign called “Think Before You Eat” and promoted vegetarianism in the documentary “Holistic Wellness.”

Additionally, Common is a dedicated advocate for HIV and AIDS awareness through his involvement in the “Knowing is Beautiful” campaign. In 2007, he made a commitment to stop using anti-gay lyrics in his music.

Beyond his music and advocacy work, Common established the Common Ground Foundation, a non-profit organization with a mission to empower underprivileged youth, helping them become strong global leaders.

The foundation offers programs in leadership development, education, creative expression, and even operates a book club.

Personal Life

Common has been in the public eye for his romantic relationships with various celebrities, including Serena Williams.

However, in August 2020, it became official that he was dating comedian and actress Tiffany Haddish.

Common Net Worth, Awards and Nominations

He holds a revered position as one of hip-hop’s most acclaimed and respected artists.

Common’s influence on the genre has been profound, and he remains a potent advocate for social and political transformation.

Common has won numerous awards throughout his career, including:

  • Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance (2003)
  • Grammy Award for Best Rap Song (2005)
  • Grammy Award for Best Rap Album (2008)
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song (2015)
  • Academy Award for Best Original Song (2015)

He has also been nominated for several other awards, including:

  • BET Award for Best Male Hip Hop Artist (2003, 2005, 2008)
  • MTV Video Music Award for Best Hip-Hop Video (2003, 2005, 2008)
  • American Music Award for Favorite Male Rap/Hip-Hop Artist (2005, 2008)
