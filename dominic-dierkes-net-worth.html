Early Beginnings and Education

Dominic’s comedy career began in his early teens when he started performing stand-up comedy at just 14 years old.

Growing up in Memphis, Tennessee, his early exposure to the comedy scene was nurtured under the supportive watch of his parents, given his underage status at the time.

His passion for comedy led him to the prestigious Tisch School of the Arts, where he majored in film and television production, a foundation that would serve him well throughout his career.

At Tisch, Dominic immersed himself in the vibrant New York comedy scene, joining the sketch comedy group Hammerkatz NYU. It was here that he met future Derrick Comedy collaborators Donald Glover and DC Pierson.

Rise with Derrick Comedy

Dominic’s involvement with Derrick Comedy marked a significant chapter in his career. The group gained widespread attention for their innovative sketch videos on YouTube, blending humor with a unique take on everyday situations and social commentary.

Their success online eventually led to the creation of the feature film Mystery Team in 2009, which Dominic co-wrote and starred in. This film became a cult favorite and showcased his skills not only as a performer but also as a creative writer.

Television and Writing Career

Following his success with Derrick Comedy, Dominic expanded his horizons into television. He became a writer for ONN, the television branch of the satirical newspaper The Onion.

His ability to craft sharp, satirical content translated well to the small screen, where he also made appearances on popular TV shows such as Parks and Recreation, Community, Mad Men, and Weeds.

In 2011, Dominic, along with his writing partner Sean Clements, ventured into animated television, contributing as writers to the short-lived Fox series Allen Gregory.

They later joined the writing staff of Workaholics for its third season, showcasing their versatility and ability to adapt to different comedic formats.

Dominic’s role in Workaholics extended beyond writing, as he guest-starred in the memorable episode Webcam Girl.
