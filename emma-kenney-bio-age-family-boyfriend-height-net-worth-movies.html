Emma Kenney Biography

Emma Kenney is an American actress who rose to fame for starring in shows like Shameless where she was cast as the daughter of William H. Macy’s character, Frank Gallagher, The Conners, and Roseanne. She has appeared in an ad for Scholastic and has directed the short film The New Girl in Town.

Additionally, Emma developed a passion for acting when she was very young and started acting at the age of 5. Her parent agreed and supported her by sending her to acting school at a very young age. Her first-ever and most successful show was Lyre Liar. She got many invites from New York University to play child roles in students’ films.

Moreover, her four-minute movie, The New Girl In Town, made it to the New Jersey International Film Festival Finals where her friends were the characters. During this time, she was nine years old and hence the youngest director in the festival’s history.
