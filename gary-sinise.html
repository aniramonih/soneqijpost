    Gary Sinise Facts

  • He grew up in Highland Park, a northern suburb of Chicago.
  • He started playing guitar when he was in the 4th grade.
  • In high school, Gary was a member of a rock band called Half Day Road.
  • Though Gary struggled academically, he loved acting during high school and participated in plays.
  • His first play was a school production of the play ‘West Side Story‘. He was cast as a gang member.
  • After high school, Gary founded the Steppenwolf Theatre Company along with his friends Terry Kinney and Jeff Perry in 1974.
  • He is a loyal fan of the Chicago Cubs and the Chicago Bears.
  • He also loves the rock band, Kansas.
  • In 2003, Amherst College in Amherst, Massachusetts awarded him with an honorary Doctorate of Humane Letters.
  • On December 10, 2008, he received the U.S. Presidential Citizens Medal for his exemplary service for the nation.
  • He and his wife, Moira Sinise, have acted together in films like Crime Story (1986), Miles from Home (1988), and Of Mice and Men (1992).
  • In 1997, as Gary received an Emmy Award for “Outstanding Lead Actor in a Miniseries or a Movie” for portraying the character of George Wallace, the real George Wallace passed away the same night.
  • He is known for playing cops in films and TV series.
  • Gary (born on March 17, 1955) shares the same birth date, March 17 (of 1936), with the original Apollo 13 pilot, Ken Mattingly, whom he portrayed in the film, Apollo 13 (1995).
  • In 2004, he founded Operation Iraqi Children with author Laura Hillenbrand.
  • While most of the main cast members of the TV series, CSI: NY (2004-2013), were from New York, Gary, Melina Kanakaredes, and Anna Belknap were the only exceptions. However, all 3 of them had also lived in New York in the past.
  • He has only used the credit “Gary A. Sinise” against the 2 episodes of Crime Story (1987) that he directed.
  • He has played the character of Detective Mac Taylor three different times in series including CSI: Crime Scene Investigation (in 2013), CSI: Miami (in 2004 & 2005), and CSI: NY (2004-2013).
  • As a band member of the Lt. Dan Band, Gary has performed in various countries including in the United States, Belgium, Japan, Korea, the Netherlands, Iraq, and Afghanistan.
  • He is a Republican. However, during the 2016 Presidential Election, he refused to vote or show support for Republican candidate Donald Trump.
  • He has acted alongside Tom Hanks in films including Forrest Gump (1994), Apollo 13 (1995), and The Green Mile (1999).
  • With Ed Harris, he has appeared in films including The Stand (1994), Apollo 13 (1995), and The Human Stain (2003).
  • On April 17, 2017, he was awarded the star of Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • As a director, he has contributed in the making of Of Mice and Men (1992), Miles from Home (1988), an episode in China Beach (1991), and 2 episodes of Thirtysomething (1989), and Crime Story (1987).
  • As a producer, he has contributed in the making of Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders (in 2016-2017), CSI: NY (in 2005-2013), Impostor (2001), and Of Mice and Men (1992).
  • In 2011, he founded the charity organization Gary Sinise Foundation to support military defenders, veterans, first responders, and their family members.
  • Visit his official website @ garysinisefoundation.org.
