Jacky Oh Autopsy Results: What is Her Cause of Death?

Jacky Oh participated in the US sketch comedy and rap battle improv game Wild ‘N Out, which Nick Cannon created and hosted for five seasons. Together with rapper and comedian DC Young Fly, she had three small children. The news of her passing shocked her fans and all wanted to know the reason behind her death.

What Was Found in Jacky Oh’s Autopsy Report?

According to an autopsy report, she passed away due to complications from cosmetic surgery.

According to the report from the Miami-Dade Medical Examiner’s office, Oh flew to Florida from Georgia on May 30 to undergo a gluteal augmentation procedure in Miami.

The procedure is commonly referred to as a butt lift. Following surgery, Oh was prescribed three drugs: the analgesic oxycodone, the antibiotic ciprofloxacin and ondansetron an antibiotic, a painkiller and an anti-nausea medication.

However, on May 31, when Oh experienced a headache and went back to the doctor for a follow-up appointment, she stopped taking ondansetron.

The autopsy report reveals that she had been told to take ibuprofen. She later experienced a burning sensation in her brain and started having trouble speaking. Emergency services were contacted by her aunt, who had traveled with her to Florida, but Oh was already unconscious when they arrived.

At 11 p.m. that evening, Oh was ultimately declared dead at the HCA Florida Mercy Hospital. Oh’s death was determined to be an accident. The Miami-Dade Police Department informed Nexstar that no criminal investigation will take place as a result.
