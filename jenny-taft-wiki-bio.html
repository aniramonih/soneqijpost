Jenny Taft Early Life

Jenny Taft was born on September 5, 1987, in Edina, Minnesota. She was born in a Christian family belonging to the White Caucasian Ethnicity. She is the daughter of John Taft, a former professional ice hockey player who personified his country in the Winter Olympics. While her mother, Mary Taft is a former speed skating champion. Taft was brought up in the United States with her younger brother named Charlie Taft. She did her studies at Edina High School, Edina, Minnesota, United States. Taft then attended Boston University, where she played lacrosse. She majored in Broadcast Journalism and graduated with a bachelor’s degree in 2010. Born in a sports family, Jenny developed her interest in sports at a very early age.

Jenny Taft Career

Jenny Taft’s broadcasting career began with Fox Sports in 2011. She served as a Fox Sports North sideline reporter for the Minnesota Lynx of the WNBA. During this, she was also a social media contributor for Minnesota’s Timberwolves, Twins, and Wild sports teams, as well as an in-stadium/arena host at Twins and Wild games. In August 2013, she was promoted to join FS1’s news and highlights update team. Jenny Taft also served as a reporter for the U.S. women’s national soccer team at the 2015 FIFA Women’s World Cup. In 2017, Taft was elevated to lead college football sideline reporter, alongside play-by-play commentator Gus Johnson and analyst Joel Klatt. In 2018, she served as a fill-in host and moderator on various FS1 programs. She retained her college football sideline reporting duties for the 2018 season. In 2020, she was a sideline reporter for Fox Sports’ coverage of XFL games.
