Early Life

Jewel Kilcher, born on May 23, 1974, in Payson, Utah, had a childhood filled with unique experiences that shaped her into the talented artist she is today. Growing up in Anchorage, Alaska, in a Mormon household, Jewel eventually chose a different path and left the Mormon faith. Her upbringing was marked by a modest lifestyle, as she and her family lived without indoor plumbing.

However, Jewel’s father, Atz Kilcher, a musician himself, played a pivotal role in her early development as a musician. He nurtured her musical talents and taught her how to yodel, among other skills. Together, they performed in local taverns and hotels, honing Jewel’s craft and passion for music.

Education was also a crucial part of Jewel’s early life. She earned a partial scholarship to attend the prestigious Interlochen Arts Academy in Michigan, where she studied operatic voice and guitar. Immersed in a nurturing environment, Jewel further refined her musical abilities and gained a deeper understanding of her craft.

After completing her studies, Jewel embarked on a journey to pursue her dreams in San Diego, California. Despite facing initial hardships, such as living in her car, she persevered, performing in coffee shops and bars to make ends meet.

Jewel’s early life experiences, combined with her raw talent and determination, laid the foundation for her future success as an acclaimed singer-songwriter and multi-talented artist.
