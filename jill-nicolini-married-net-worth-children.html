Is Jill Nicolini Married? Her Age, Net Worth, Family, Children &amp; Facts

Published Date: 02 Feb, 2021 Updated date: 02 Feb, 2021 By Daniel Roffer

Jill Nicolini is a multi-talented personality who has been active in the broadcasting media for nearly two decades. She is a prominent face when it comes to weather broadcasting and entertainment reporting.

Jill Nicolini is an American reporter, former model, actress, and television personality. She now serves as an anchor and reporter on WPIX 11 Morning News in New York City.

Jill Nicolini Bio; Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Born as Jill Marie Nicolini in 1978, she is 43 years of age. She celebrates her birthday on the 10th of January every year.

She was raised by her parents Anthony Nicolini and mother Michelle R. Nicolini. She has a sister named Jennifer Rodriguez and a brother named Anthony Nicolini. She grew up with her siblings in Farmingville, New York, the United States.


Jill possesses a mixed ethnicity. According to wiki sources, she is of Italian heritage from her family side. She stands at a height of 5 feet 6 inches (1.68 meters).

Her Education; High School, College

Jill attended Sachem North High School in Lake Ronkonkoma, New York, and graduated at age 18 in 1996. Afterward, she went to Stony Brook University in Stony Brook, New York, and studied pre-medicine for a year.

Moreover, she earned her Associate degree in Communication and Media Studies from Suffolk County Community College in 1999. She graduated with a Master’s degree in Communication and Media Studies from the New York Institute of Technology in 2001.

Her Career

Jill began her media career after joining WBLI Radio in Long Island in 1997. Moreover, she reported for LI News Tonight while at NYIT.

In 2001, she became the arena announcer for the New York Islanders. Later, she became an on-camera traffic reporter at Long Island’s Metro & Traffic and Weather Channel and News 12/Long Island.

Nicolini served as the weekday morning traffic reporter at WPIX from 2004 to 2011. During that time, she served as an entertainment anchor and feature reporter.


In 2011, she joined WNYW Fox 5 as an entertainment reporter. Likewise, she served as a traffic reporter and fill-in host on Live From The Couch on WLNY-TV. She also worked as a traffic reporter for CBS 2 News This Morning on WCBS-TV.

In 2017, she returned to WPIX and now serves as a fill-in traffic reporter. Moreover, she is an on-site reporter for the station’s coverage of Giants and Jets games.
