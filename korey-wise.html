The Complete Story of Central Park Jogger Case

The incident took place on the night of April 19, 1989, in the North Woods of Manhattan’s Central Park. It involved rape and assault of Trisha Meili (a white female jogger). She was a 28-year-old investment banker at the time and the attack left her in a coma for 12 days.

Furthermore, in 1990, the police brought Raymond Santana, Antron McCray, Kevin Richardson, Yusef Salaam, and Korey as suspects of the crime. The juveniles were taken for various interview sessions for hours.

Santana, McCray, and Kevin gave their statements in the presence of their respective parents or guardians. According to a source, Wise made a number of statements on his own. Except him, all other four confessed to a number of the attacks committed in the park that night.

Later, the defendants were accused of rape, sexual abuse, assault, robbery, riot,and attempted murder. They also charged for other attacks in the park, based solely on confessions that they told were forced and false.

In December 1990, Wise was acquitted of those charges. But he convicted of sexual abuse, assault, and riot and sentenced to 5–15 years. The four younger boys, Antron, Richardson, Salaam, and Santana served their time in juvenile facilities before being transferred to adult jails at age 21.

However, in 2002, Matias Reyes (convicted murderer and serial rapist) who was serving a life sentence in prison confessed to raping Trisha Meili. His DNA also matched with the rape kit of the jogger.

Finally, all the wrongly convicted teenagers released then. Later, they sued New York City in 2003 for malicious prosecution, racial discrimination, and emotional distress. Moreover, the city concluded the case for $41 million USD in 2014 in damages from New York State in the New York Court of Claims.
