Kyle Graper of Pittsburgh, Technology Support Specialist at Jones Day, suddenly passes away

Pittsburgh, PA – Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, is grieving the tragic loss of Kyle Graper, a dedicated community advocate and technology support specialist at Jones Day. Originally from Whitehall, Pennsylvania, Kyle was a passionate individual known for his relentless pursuit of communal unity and advocacy for equal rights.

Sisters Place shared the Kyle Graper’s death news and wrote,

“Sisters Place mourns the loss of a longtime Friends Board member, Kyle Graper. Kyle was an integral committee member for Crushed Grapes for longer than most of us can remember – at least 10 years. He was in charge of logistics, and was there first thing in the morning, until the last person left in the evening, often talking excitedly about how well it went and what we can do better the next year. We will miss his enthusiasm, energy, and passion – he was always there when we needed him. Rest well, dear friend.
A memorial will be held in October. Thoughts and prayers to his family and friends.”

Kyle’s impact extended beyond his career, as he actively engaged in philanthropy, art, and creativity. He dedicated a significant portion of his life to activism, utilizing his platform to champion various causes. An avid tabletop gamer, he cherished spending time with his extensive network of friends and participated in paintball tournaments, showcasing his love for adventure.

A movie enthusiast, Kyle frequented local theaters and was a respected costumer, leaving a lasting mark in communities like the 501st Legions. He shared a profound passion for animals, particularly cats, rescuing them from local shelters and providing them with a safe and loving home.

Despite battling Crohn’s disease, Kyle never allowed it to define or limit him, embodying resilience and strength. He co-hosted the podcast “Thieves, Rogues, and Renegades” and was an integral member of the 501st Legion, utilizing the Star Wars platform for philanthropy to raise awareness about childhood illness.

In his hometown of Whitehall, Kyle was remembered as a former Lifeguard and Operations Supervisor at Dorney Park & Wildwater Kingdom. He took pride in his community roots and remained committed to bettering the lives of those around him.

Furthermore, Kyle’s compassionate nature extended to organizing charitable events like “Crushed Grapes,” an annual wine-toasting fundraiser benefiting Sister’s Place. His dedication and tireless efforts in supporting causes he believed in left an indelible mark on all who knew him.

A memorial to honor Kyle Graper is set to take place in October, offering an opportunity for family and friends to pay their respects and celebrate his impactful life. Pittsburgh and Whitehall communities join in extending their thoughts and prayers to Kyle’s family and loved ones during this difficult time.
