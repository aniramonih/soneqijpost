American Idol Star Lucy Love Wiki | Bio

Lucy Love (age: 28 years; born in 1994) is a singer, songwriter, and comedian from Holly Grove, Arkansas, who rose to fame in 2023 after making it into the “American Idol” season 21. Love auditioned from Las Vegas and sang Marvin Gaye’s “I Heard It Through the Grapevine” during the audition.

Lionel, Katy, and Luke watched her intently as she sang, trying to assess her talent and potential. However, after she finished, Lionel expressed his concern that her performance was too comedic. Criticism is always hard to hear, but Love did not let it affect her.

Instead, she took Lionel’s feedback positively and showcased her true vocal abilities by singing a second song. She sang “A Song for You” by Donny Hathaway this time. As soon as she started singing, the judges were blown away. Richie clapped for Lucy when she finished singing and told her how impressively she performed. All the judges said yes to her and then gave her the golden ticket to move to the next round.

Lucy Love’s Performance in Hollywood Week

After impressing the judges in Las Vegas, she moved to the next round of the competition – Hollywood Week. Lucy knew she needed to work on her stage presence to stand out in the competition.

She spent many hours rehearsing and perfecting her performance skills, determined to give it her all in front of the judges. For her first performance in Hollywood Week, Lucy sang “Make You Feel My Love” by Bob Dylan.

As she sang, she could feel the energy in the room – the judges were captivated by her voice and stage presence. But Lucy didn’t stop there. She was then picked to be a duet partner by Elijah McCormick, and together, they formed the “Dunamis Duo.”

Their performance of “My Girl” by The Temptations was electric, showcasing their chemistry and talent as a duo. For her final solo performance, Lucy sang “Flying Without Wings.” It was a risky choice, but Lucy knew she needed to push herself to stand out from the crowd. And once again, she delivered a stunning performance that left the judges in awe.
