Career

Sharapova started attending a tennis clinic in Moscow, which was run by Martina Navratilova, who recommended professional training at the IMG Academy in Bradenton, Florida. Later Sharapova moved to the United States of America, where she was trained under Rick Macci.

Her first hit at the tennis court was in November 2000 when she won the Eddie Herr International Junior Championships in the girls 16 at the age of 13, and she was also awarded a special award called The Rising Star Award, which was awarded only to players who are extraordinarily promising.

She very soon reached No. 6 in the ITF junior world singles ranking in 2002. Her best breakthrough was the finals of the 2002 Australian Open, third round of the 2002 French Open, second round of the 2001 U.S Open and finals of the 2002 Wimbledon Championships. The 2004 Wimbledon Championship rose Sharapova to fame.

Following this victory, she won six matches in her preparation for the U.S Open. Sharapova’s ranking to world no 1 was really stupendous with all her performances of the extraordinary level at French Open, US Open, Australian Open, and Wimbledon Championships.
