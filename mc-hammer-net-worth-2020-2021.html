Career

Mc hammer started his career at the age of 11 when he performed dance routines outside Oakland Coliseum during A’s home games. He later caught the attention of the team’s owner Chuck Finley and was invited to watch a game from his luxury box. Finley got fond of the hammer and hired him as the team’s batboy; even as he dreamed of playing professional baseball, the hammer never turned his back on music. While playing for the A’s, he adopted the moniker ‘MC’ for the master of ceremonies and performed at various clubs when the A’s traveled out of town.

He acquired the name ‘Hammer’ from his resemblance with ‘The Hammer,’ Aaron. Hammer was a talented baseball player in high school; he earned himself a tryout with the San Francisco Giants but failed to make it to the finals. After a short stint in college and three years in the navy where he worked as an aviation storekeeper, the hammer returned to Oakland and began performing in clubs. He later formed a Christian rap group with Jon Gibson known as Holy Ghost Boys, one of their songs; ‘The Wall’ became a hit. Former A’s players Mike Davis And Dwayne Murphy helped him financially to start his own record label, ‘Burst It Records.’

In 1987, he released his debut album feel my power, and later ‘Ring ‘Em.’ In 1988 he earned a multi-album deal with capital records. On February 12, 1990, his next album, please hammer, don’t hurt me was released.
