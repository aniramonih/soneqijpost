Michelle Valles Biography

Michelle Valles is an American journalist who was born and brought up in El Paso, Texas, United States of America. Currently, she works at NBC4 in Los Angeles, California, as a general assignment reporter weekdays for NBC4’s Today and the NBC4 News at 11 a.m. She also reports and anchors the weekend edition of Today in LA from 7:00 to 8:00 a.m. Previously Michelle was an evening news anchor and reporter with the NBC and CBS affiliates in the Austin and El Paso markets.

    Michelle Valles NBC4 News

    Valles co-anchors the NBC4 News at 11 a.m. In addition, she can be seen at Today in LA, the weekend morning news from 7:00 to 8:00 a.m. The broadcast journalist works occasionally as a general assignment reporter at the station during the week. Joining the station in 2012, her most recent news stories have been posted on social media.

    She interviews journalists, writers, dignitaries, and other local experts as a co-anchor to address issues with greater depth and clarification. As one of her most unforgettable roles at NBC4, she points to her reporting of the death of Rodney King.

    In her career, she has also covered a two-part investigation on Americans being unfairly detained in a Mexican prison; the notorious Juarez murders; and Mexican orphanage conditions among many other stories.

    She was also inspired by her candid and exclusive interview with Willie Nelson about her use of marijuana and her attempts to decriminalize the drug in the U.S. Valles is voted Best Anchor by the Texas Associated Press, the Austin Chronicle, and the Rare Magazine. 

    The journalist was also part of the news team that won the Texas Associated Press Best Newscast Award in 2007 and was chosen for seven consecutive years by the Austin American statesman to be included in their prestigious Fortunate 500 community service list.

    In addition to her work as a broadcast journalist, she featured nationally on the HBO Latino series Habla Texas which she also co-created, wrote, and also co-host the hour lifestyle show Austin Live.

    Other interests include acting and writing. As of her acting, Valles was featured in The Lying Game, When Angels Sing, and A Ride with Bob. She also wrote a column for the magazine, Austin Man, providing a woman’s perspective on men’s issues.
