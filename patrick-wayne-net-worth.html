Patrick Wayne Wiki Biography

Born on the 15th of July, 1939 in Los Angeles, California, Patrick John Morrison, professionally known as Patrick Wayne, is the son of the legendary actor John Wayne. Patrick has followed in his father’s footsteps, appearing in over 70 film and TV titles, including noteworthy appearances in “Rio Grande” (1950), “The Bears and I” (1974), and “Sinbad and the Eye of the Tiger” (1977), among others.

Patrick Wayne’s net worth has been estimated to be around $12 million as of mid-2016. He earned this substantial amount through his extensive career in acting, as well as through his role as a television host on shows like “The Monte Carlo Show” and “Tic-Tac-Dough”.

Patrick Wayne Net Worth $12 Million

Patrick, the son of John Wayne and Josephine Alicia Saenz, earned a degree from Loyola Marymount University in 1961. Even before completing his education, Patrick had already ventured into the entertainment industry, making his debut in the film “Rio Grande” alongside his father. This was followed by appearances in eight more films with John Wayne, such as “The Quiet Man” and “The Alamo”, significantly impacting Patrick’s net worth.

In 1959, Patrick starred in the film “The Young Land” and continued to land roles in various films, including “Cheyenne Autumn” in 1964 and “Shenandoah” in 1965, further boosting his net worth. Throughout the 1970s, he continued to build on his success, appearing in productions such as “The Bears and I” (1974), “The People That Time Forgot” (1977), and the TV series “Shirley” (1979-1980).

Transitioning to television in the 1980s, Patrick appeared in several series and game shows, including “Fantasy Island” (1981-1983), “Love Boat” (1979-1986), and “Murder, She Wrote” (1987). He also made sporadic appearances on both TV and the big screen before retiring in 1997. Notable productions during this phase include “Kung Fu: The Legend Continues” (1995), “High Tide” (1997), and “Deep Cover” (1997).

Beyond his acting skills, Patrick Wayne garnered numerous nominations and awards, including the Golden Globe Award for Most Promising Newcomer for his role in “The Searchers” (1956). After retiring from acting, he dedicated himself to philanthropy, assuming the role of chairman at the John Wayne Cancer Institute since 2003.

In his personal life, Patrick Wayne was married to Peggy Hunt from 1965 until 1978, with whom he had three children. In 1999, he married Misha Anderson, and they continue to be together.
