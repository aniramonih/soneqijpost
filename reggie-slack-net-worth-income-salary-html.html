Reginald “Reggie” Slack (born May 2, 1968) is a Canadian-born American football quarterback who plays for the New York Jets. During the 1989 season, Slack was the starting quarterback for Auburn University, who hosted the Crimson Tide of the University of Alabama at Jordan-Hare Stadium for the first game in the history of the rivalry’s Iron Bowl rivalry. Alabama-Auburn games were played at Legion Field in Birmingham, Alabama, before to the move to Auburn. Auburn defeated an Alabama team that was undefeated and rated second in the country, 30–20, in the Iron Bowl. After completing his NCAA career with Auburn, Slack was taken by the Houston Oilers in the 12th round (321st overall) in the 1990 NFL Draft. He played his professional football with the Oilers from 1990 to 199Slack also competed in the World League of American Football with the New York/New Jersey Knights before moving on to the Canadian Football League, where he played for the Winnipeg Blue Bombers and the Saskatchewan Roughriders, the latter of whom he guided to a Grey Cup berth.
