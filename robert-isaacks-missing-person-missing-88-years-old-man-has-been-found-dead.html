Robert Isaacks Missing Person, Missing 88 Years Old Man Has Been Found Dead

Robert Isaacks Missing Person, Death – In a tragic turn of events, the Bandera County Sheriff’s Office has confirmed the identity of the body discovered on November 17 as 88-year-old Robert Isaacks, who had been reported missing just two days prior. The elderly man was residing with his aunt in Bandera and was last seen entering the home on surveillance footage around 10 a.m. on November 15, accompanied by a woman identified as Freda M. Thomas, also known as Michelle.

According to authorities, an autopsy conducted in Crockett County substantiated Isaacks’ identity and raised concerns about the circumstances surrounding his disappearance. The Bandera County Sheriff’s Office has now issued a murder warrant for Freda M. Thomas, urging the community’s assistance in locating her. Isaacks’ granddaughter, Stephanie Holan, expressed bewilderment, stating that her family had no prior knowledge of Thomas, emphasizing the urgency of apprehending her.

Law enforcement officials are cautioning the public not to approach Thomas and to prioritize their safety by contacting the Bandera County Sheriff’s Office at (830) 796-3771 with any relevant information. The issuance of a murder warrant underscores the severity of the situation, heightening concerns about the potential danger posed by Thomas. The plea for community assistance emphasizes the collaborative effort needed to bring justice to this case and ensure the safety of others.

As the investigation unfolds, the Bandera County Sheriff’s Office is relying on tips and information from the public to piece together the events leading to Isaacks’ tragic demise. The community’s vigilance and cooperation are instrumental in swiftly resolving this case and providing closure to the grieving family mourning the loss of their elderly relative under distressing circumstances.
