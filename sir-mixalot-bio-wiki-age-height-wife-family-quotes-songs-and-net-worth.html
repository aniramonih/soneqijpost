    Mixalot Career

    Sir had an ear and a passion for music. After high school, he starts DJing parties at local community centers. He had started playing weekends regularly at the Rainier Vista Boys and Girls Club in South Seattle By 1983. He moved locations and began throwing his parties at the Rotary Boys and Girls Club in the Central District. It was there that he met Nasty Nes Rodriguez, a local radio DJ and host of Fresh Tracks, the West Coast’s first rap radio show on Seattle station KKFX (K-Fox).
    He partnered with Nasty Nes and local businessman Ed Locke to found the Nastymix record label in the year 1983. The first song to receive fame outside of Seattle was Square Dance Rap in the year 1986. He had originally decided to rap the entire song slow, and speed and pitch it up in upload production, Mix later told Seattle Refined in the year 2018 that he didn’t want to rap, that’s why I use this weird Smurf voice. After the song was hit up by DJs in clubs nationwide, he went touring in Florida, New York, and other states. While in Arizona, he realized a street named Broadway with a restaurant labeled Dick’s, just such as Seattle. This gave him the idea to write his next top Posse on Broadway.
    In the year 1987, Mix-a-Lot’s next top release was the single Posse on Broadway, whose name referred to Broadway in Seattle’s Capitol Hill district. The song made the hit 100 but quickly disappeared, although it remains famous in the Seattle area for its references to several local landmarks.
    Swass, Sir Mix-a-Lot’s debut album, was released in the year 1988 with two other singles; Square-Dance Rapand Iron Man, a rap metal track sampling from the Black Sabbath song of the same title, which was backed by the band Metal Church. The Recording Industry Association of America certified Swass platinum in the year 1990. The seminar, released in the year 1989 featured My Hooptie, Beepers, Gortex and I Got Game.
