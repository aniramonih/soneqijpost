Twinkie Clark Bio (Wiki)

Who is Twinkie Clark?

Twinkie Clark is an American gospel artist, record producer, songwriter, musician, and evangelist. Twinkie Clark is best known as a part of the American gospel music group ‘Clark Sisters.’

How many siblings does Twinkie Clark have?

Twinkie was born Elbernita Twinkie Clark on November 15, 1954. Her zodiac sign is Scorpio and she’s 68 years old.

She was born to pioneer gospel singer and choral director Dr. Mattie Moss Clark and Elder Elbert Clark in Detroit, Michigan.

Twinkie Clark has four sisters, Jacky Clark Chisholm, Denise “Niecy” Clark-Bradford, Dorinda Clark-Cole, and Karen Clark Sheard. Twinkie started her music lessons under the supervision of her mother, very early in life.

In addition, from the age of thirteen, she started to travel nationwide with her mum, ministering and practicing choirs in three-part vocal harmony.

Twinkie Clark received formal music learning at “Howard University” in Washington, D.C.

Twinkie Clark: Professional Life, Career

in 1967, Twinkie Clark made her debut as a featured vocalist alongside her mother, her sister Denise Clark, and Dolores Jones on the Southwest Michigan State Choir of Church Of God in Christ’s “He Will Provide Your Need” album, A Deeper Walk with Thee.

Twinkie Clark is popular as the “Mother of Contemporary Gospel Music.” Besides singing, she’s a well-known musician.

She is also popular as the “Queen of the B3 Hammond Organ,” moreover she is also a member of the 2014 Hammond Organ Hall of Fame.

The Clark Sisters’ Became the Highest-Rated TV Movie in 2020

The Clark Sisters: First Ladies of Gospel movie broadcast on Lifetime became the most highly-rated TV movie in 2020.

The movie had an incredible 2.7 million viewers right after airing and has been Lifetime’s most highly-rated movie since 2016.

In addition, the film is based on the real-life story of the Clark Sisters, and how they founded the gospel group ‘The Clark Sisters,’ which rose to fame. And it helped bring contemporary gospel music to the audience.

Similarly, the film reveals how the Sisters made history, becoming the highest-selling female group in Gospel history, their hard work.

And accomplishments over the years have made them very popular in the gospel music industry.

Solo career

Twinkie Clark has released a number of solo albums. She released her debut studio albums, Glory Belongs to God (1979), Comin’ Home and The Masterpiece (1996), Twinkie Clark-Terrell Performs the Florida A&M University Gospel Choir, Twinkie Clark & Friends…Live in Charlotte, which was nominated for Grammy for Best Classic Soul Gospel Record.

In addition, in 2011 and 2013, Larry Clark Gospel launched With Humility and Live & Unplugged, respectively, until the release of Larry Clark’s collaboration album The Generations in 2020.

Twinkie Clark: Awards, Nominations

The Grammy Awards are presented annually by the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences. Clark won 2 awards out of 8 nominations.

The NAACP Image Awards are presented annually by the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP). Clark won 1 award out of 3 nominations.

Every year The Soul Train Music Awards are held. Clark has won three nominations.
