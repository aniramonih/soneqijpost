What is Warren Sapp’s Net Worth and Salary?

Warren Sapp, the retired American football player, is known for his hard-hitting style of play during his 13-season career in the NFL, predominantly for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. However, Warren’s reputation was marred by controversies and verbal abuse towards other players.

Despite earning nearly $60 million in NFL salary alone, in 2012 Warren declared bankruptcy with reported debt of $6 million. As a result, his current net worth is estimated at $500 thousand.

Early Life and Athletic Career

Warren Carr Sapp was born on December 19th of 1972 in Orlando, Florida and raised by his single mother in Plymouth, Florida. He showed his athletic potential after joining his school football team at Apopka High School, where he played multiple positions including tight end, linebacker, punter, and placekicker. Sapp set various records during his high school years, including most tackles for a loss, most sacks, and the longest field goal. He also played basketball for his high school.

After graduating from high school, Warren Sapp played as a defensive standout at the University of Miami. His coach converted him into a defensive lineman, leading to multiple awards. He won the Bronko Nagurski Trophy in 1994 for Best Defensive Player, the Rotary Lombardi Award for Best Lineman, and the Bill Willis Award for Best Defensive Lineman.

Warren Sapp’s Career Highlights

Warren Sapp was drafted 12th overall by the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in 1995 and played for them as a right defensive tackle throughout his stay in Tampa. He had a promising first season, with one interception and 27 tackles, and improved notably in 1997 with 51 tackles and 9 sacks.

In 2004, Sapp signed a 7-year contract worth $36.6 million with the Oakland Raiders. He played both defensive end and defensive tackle, recording 30 tackles, 2.5 sacks, and 2 fumble recoveries in 16 games. The following season, he started as a defensive tackle and had 29 tackles and 5 sacks before being sidelined due to a shoulder injury.

Returning in 2006, Sapp recorded 10 sacks and 32 tackles. He had 37 tackles and 2 sacks in the 2007 season but was ejected from a game in Jacksonville after an altercation with officials. On March 4, 2008, Sapp confirmed his retirement.

Sapp was one of only 12 defensive players in NFL history to make the Pro Bowl, win a Super Bowl, and be honored as Defensive Player of the Year. He participated in 7 Pro Bowls and was named a first-team All-Pro 4 times and second-team All-Pro 2 times. He was named Defensive Player of the Year after his 1999 season.

Warren Sapp’s NFL Salary

Warren Sapp signed a contract extension for $36 million over 6 years in 1998. In 2004, he agreed to a 7-year contract with the Oakland Raiders for $36.6 million. Sapp retired in 2008 before playing out the full contract. He earned a total of $58 million in NFL salary throughout his career.

Controversies Involving Warren Sapp

During a 2002 game, Sapp injured Chad Clifton of the Green Bay Packers, leaving him hospitalized for a week and affecting his ability to walk for several weeks. Sapp was caught on camera verbally abusing a Packers coach who approached him about the hit.

Sapp was involved in heated arguments with Jerome Bettis of the Pittsburgh Steelers in 2002 and disrupted a Colts warmup in 2003. He also ran into an NFL referee before a game against the Washington Redskins and was fined $50,000.

In 2007, Sapp was ejected from a game and fined $75,000 for repeatedly mouthing off at officials during a penalty dispute.

Legal Troubles

In February 2010, Sapp was arrested in South Florida for domestic battery, although the charges were later dropped. Five years later, in February 2015, he was arrested again for suspicion of soliciting a prostitute and assault, which led to the termination of his contract with the NFL Network. However, the charges were dismissed in May of the same year.

Warren Sapp’s Financial Troubles

In 2010, Warren Sapp’s financial troubles began. He was awarded a judgment of almost $1 million and had a monthly lien of $33,333 filed against his NFL Network paycheck of $45,000. He also owed money to the IRS, unpaid property taxes, alimony, child support, and various attorneys and friends. This all added up to a debt of over $1.8 million.

In April 2012, Sapp filed for bankruptcy, claiming to have lost his championship rings from his time with the Buccaneers and University of Miami. By this time, his debts totaled $6.7 million, while his assets were valued at $6.45 million.

Warren Sapp’s Former Florida Mansion Sold for Less than Half its Value

Warren Sapp, who filed for bankruptcy, had to sell his 15,162 square-foot luxury home in Windermere, Florida. The mansion was once valued at $7 million, but unfortunately, it was sold at auction for just $2.9 million in November 2012.

Despite the low sale price, a video tour of the property was still available online years later, showcasing the grandeur of the former NFL player’s once-luxurious home.
