Who is Lauren Daigle?

Lauren Ashley Daigle, born on September 9, 1991, is an American singer and songwriter known for her contemporary Christian music. She signed with Centricity Music and made her debut in 2015 with the release of her album "How Can It Be."

Article continues below advertisementArticle continues below advertisement

This album achieved remarkable success, reaching No. 1 on the Billboard Top Christian Albums chart and earning Platinum certification from the RIAA. It also produced three No. 1 singles on the Billboard Christian Airplay chart, namely "First," "Trust in You," and "O'Lord."

Daigle's third studio album, "Look Up Child," was released in September 2018 and became a significant milestone in her career. The album's popularity was bolstered by the crossover success of the single "You Say." "Look Up Child" debuted at No. 3 on the Billboard 200 chart, making it the highest-charting Christian album by a female artist in over two decades.

It also claimed the top spot on the Top Christian Albums chart, selling 115,000 album-equivalent units in its first week. The lead single, "You Say," reached No. 29 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and set a new record for the longest-running No. 1 position on the Billboard Hot Christian Songs chart, staying at the top for an impressive 129 weeks among solo artists.

Daigle's exceptional work on this album earned her two Grammy Awards. Beyond her Grammy wins, Daigle's talent has been recognized with seven GMA Dove Awards, five Billboard Music Awards, and two American Music Awards.

Her impact on the Christian music scene is further reflected in her four No. 1 singles on both the Billboard Christian Airplay and Hot Christian Songs charts. Lauren Daigle continues to captivate audiences with her soulful music and remains a prominent figure in contemporary Christian music.

How Old is Lauren Daigle?

 Lauren Daigle was 31 years old. Born in Lake Charles, Louisiana, Lauren Daigle spent her formative years in Lafayette, Louisiana.

Growing up in a vibrant musical environment, she was influenced by the sounds of zydeco, blues, and Cajun music. Daigle's love for singing was evident from a young age, earning her house the endearing nickname of "the music box" by her mother.

However, it was not until she contracted cytomegalovirus at the age of 15 that Daigle began taking music seriously. The diagnosis forced her to take a hiatus from school for two years.
