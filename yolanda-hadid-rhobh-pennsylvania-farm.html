Inside RHOBH alum Yolanda Hadids serene Pennsylvania farm featuring a garden, fire pit and horse

YOLANDA Hadid is loving life down at her serene $4 million Pennsylvania farm in her garden and with her beloved horses.

The Real Housewives Of Beverly Hills star was previously in coronavirus self-isolation on her gorgeous 32-acre property which she bought in 2017.

Yolanda, 57, bought the New Hope property to be closer to her supermodel daughters Gigi, 26, and Bella, 25, who both live in Manhattan.

The sprawling 5,000 square-foot estate features horse stables, garden, fire pit and even a botanical garden from which the former model makes her own essential oils and is developing her own line of gin.

Not only does she have horses but also, chickens, goats, sheep and Scottish highland cattle.

The stunning home is a perfect place for her daughters and son Anwar, 22, to escape from the big city and their successful modeling careers.

She once told Elle: "It’s where we can all come and feel like ourselves again.

"When I’m here, I just get in my truck and go to the store. Kids get excited at the market, but they don’t take their phones out.

"They want us to feel normal, and that’s very appreciated."

Yolanda also takes advantage of the surrounding gorgeous nature to host dinners for her friends and family.

She regularly invites people over for meals and hosts them at a long wooden table with crisp white linen chairs set all around it.

Lavender from her botanical garden adorns the tables and an Instagram post shows one of her favorite white horses poking his head out of the nearby barn.

“When you work hard and your dreams become your reality,” she captioned an Instagram photo.

She also appreciated the beauty around her: “First day of Lavender Harvest on the farm, so much gratitude for Mother Earth’s magical and healing gift bestowed upon us.”

It was tough work setting up life on the rural property, where her daily tasks include gardening, tending to the horses and farming fruits and vegetables.

But Yolanda thought it was all worth it:  “Sometimes in order to find peace, you have to disconnect from the people, places and things that no longer inspire you or grow your soul in order to live in your highest consciousness.”

Born in the Netherlands, Yolanda worked as high-flying model for 15 years before settling down real estate developer, Mohammed Hadid.

They married in 1994 and had Gigi, Bella and Anwar before divorcing in 2000.

Yolanda then married musician and producer David Foster in 2010 in a ceremony in Beverly Hills.

During their marriage she shot to national fame on Bravo's reality show RHOBH.

Yolanda and David divorced in 2017 and she went on to buy her stunning New Hope home.

But Yolanda managed to find love again when she started dating CEO Joseph Jingoli in May 2019.

It did not take long after the pair got together before Yolanda started gushing about him on Instagram.

She wrote: "Celebrating you today and every day... Thank you for being such a beautiful light in my life...... The calm in the middle of my storm and the rock in this most difficult time. Happy birthday my love, I pray that each day, love, health and happiness will come your way."

Yolanda is outspoken in raising awareness about Lyme Disease for which she has been in remission from for more than a year but still experiences flare ups.

Lyme disease is a bacterial infection, spread by ticks, which can cause rashes, headaches, muscle pain and inflammation of the spinal cord.

She published her memoir about living with the painful disease in 2017 called, Believe Me: My Battle with the Invisible Disability of Lyme Disease.

Yolanda told ABC news: "This is such an invisible disability, where you look beautiful on the outside yet you're dying on the inside."
